Study of the domain

ABOUT

Tenendoci fermi sul terreno dell’interventismo - né potrebbe essere altrimenti, essendo stato l’interventismo il fatto dominante nella storia della nazione - noi rivendichiamo il diritto e proclamiamo il dovere di trasformare, se sarà inevitabile anche con metodi rivoluzionari, la vita italiana. Chi vorrebbe dipingerci come conservatori o reazionari, semplicemente perché non abbiamo più in tasca le tessere delle varie chiese o perché non ci rassegnamo a gettare nell’Adriatico i centomila italiani della Dalmazia, è un poderoso impecille. Noi interventisti siamo I soli che in Italia hanno il diritto di parlare di rivoluzione.
Benito Mussolini, 23 marzo, “Il Popolo d’Italia”, 18 marzo 1919

Through these peremptory words, expressed through the deadliest among his political weapons - his personal newspaper -, the young Benito Mussolini asserts what he thought to be the true and authentic nature of his movement. Following the distancing, during 1914, from his first militancy experience in the Socialist Party ranks as “L’Avanti” newspaper editor, his political adventure will in a first place lead him toward the foundation, in the same year, of the interventionist newspaper “Il Popolo d’Italia”. Secondly toward the establishment of his personal “anti-party” movement called “Fasci Italiani di Combattimento” in 1919. Finally, during the troubled and tormented period of the war aftermath (1919-1922), he led his movement up to the brutal seizure of the institutional power with the “March on Rome” putsch in 1922. The historical scenario is what the liberal Italian historiographic tradition entitled “Biennio Rosso”, which covers the years 1919 and 1920; the place we are looking at is northern Italy; the historical phenomena is called “Sansepolcrism”. The WW1 is just over and Italian society, despite his entry as a winner nation at Versailles negotiation, lays in a condition of economic disaster and socio-political disaggregation. The general dissatisfaction derived from the war venture has a two-faced dimension. It is precisely on the base of the dialectic between these two dimensions that the phenomena we are considering had the possibility to arose. On the one hand the official Italian Socialist Party (PSI), which in 1914 embraced neutralist positions toward the global conflict, rides the popular anger caused by the war struggle and economic downturn resulted from it and rearrange his political organization in order to trigger, finally, a revolutionary process toward “real socialism”, following the Russian 1917’s Soviet experiment. On the other, an heterogeneous plethora of movements composed by interventionist socialist, anarcho-individualist, revolutionary syndicalists, Arditi’s militia , and Futurist, express his discontent about the war outcomes assuming irredentist position, stating that territories of Istria and Dalmatia “naturally” belongs to Italy - while Versailles conference decided differently - and must rejoin it.

The peculiar ideological traits shared by those organized irredentist groups can be summarised in a visceral distrust toward bourgeois ideology, expressed as stigmatization of liberal democracy and parliamentary system – both guilty, according to the leader perspective, of political class’s degeneration, corruption and laxity –, as well as the strict denunciation of the of the capitalistic mode of production’s structural contradictions and the official socialist party’s utopian ambition. The explosive mixture derived from the violent clash between these two political realities generated the historical condition of possibility for the emergence end the reinforcement of Mussolini’s composite movement. The decisive factor in this rise was the role played by Mussolini movement in sustaining, partly indirectly given the convergence of intentions with the constituted power – or to get rid of the “red menace” – and partly out of instrumental necessity of “real politik” practical needs, a fierce and bloody struggle for repressive purposes against the threat posed by the fascination on the popular strata of the socialist party’s propaganda and its claim of an imminent overthrow of the established order and class structure. Such conduct brings to Mussolini’s movement the sympathies of the institutions, of the conservative middle and upper classes, as well as substantial funding from finance and industry cosmos, highly concerned about the subversive climate generated by the increasingly massive adherence of the polar classes to the principles of socialism. With a markedly progressive economic policy program and a series of republican, anticlerical, anti-capitalist and anti-liberal political instances, the morning of March 23, 1919, at the “Teatro Dal Verme” in San Sepolcro square in Milan, Benito Mussolini, Ferruccio Vecchi , Filippo Marinetti and others, sanction the birth of the so-called "Fascio Primogenito", or "Fascio Italiano di Combattimento". The embryonic phase of Italian fascism season is therefore inaugurated by the experience of Sansepolcro. The movement, followed by a small electoral base purely constituted by northern Italy middle-class, will try to climb to power through institutional channels in the political elections of November 16, 1919, and will be dramatically defeated. Mussolini's political strategy therefore undergoes for a structural reorganization, and once abandoned his progressive and republican ambitions, as well as his attempt to achieve the so-called "third way” in the field of economic policy through legal and institutional ways, it turned out to an exasperated pro-monarchic nationalism reaching positions that would have been more and more conservative, in order to mobilize the most reactionary traditionalist strata of the electorate. The outcome of the movement reorganization will lead him directly to the violent seizure of power with the “March on Rome”. The fiercely revolutionary intentions triggered by the Mussolini movement fails miserably under the weight of a very low electoral consensus. The fascist era begins, an autocratic dictatorial regime that of his Sansepolcrist origins reflected nothing but the name.

Metadata

METADATA ALIGNMENT

In the Metadata Alignment’s phase, some useful properties were taken from each metadata standard identified during the Metadata Analysis.

DCTerms, even if not found in the descriptions of the items, was selected as a starter for the alignment. Dublin Core is in fact one of the most used formats on the web for describing resources and has been indicated as a standard format within the Open Archive Initiative. DCTerms extends the 15 core DC classes.

Regarding MARC and MARCXML, no distinction was made. Although they are referred to as separate standards, they actually share the same classes, just in different formats. Furthermore, MARC 21 has been inserted in the alignment table being it the most recent version and the one currently documented, which integrates some different national variants used mostly in Anglo-Saxon countries.

For accessibility reasons, all the properties have been divided into four categories: WHO, WHERE, WHEN, and WHAT.

URI Definition

Referring to the single items of the project's domain and in order to link them together inside the RDF file, the team decided to create unique names for all the entities in form of https URIs.

Moreover, some imaginary paths were designed for them: /items for the 10 items and /events, /timespans, /persons, /institutions, and /websites for the other ones, depending on their nature.
